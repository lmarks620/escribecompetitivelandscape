Granicus

Enterprise GovTech · PE-backed (Vista Equity) · 14+ acquisitions · Legistar, OneMeeting, Peak Agenda

Win Theme: Precision Over Sprawl
Snapshot

Granicus is the incumbent govtech giant — built through 14+ acquisitions under Vista Equity's PE playbook. They offer a broad suite (agenda management, streaming, FOIA, CRM, websites) but the products were never truly integrated. A former Granicus VP described the portfolio as "vendor sprawl reduction" that is actually "a patchwork of acquisitions." Their generalist sales restructuring — one rep selling everything from websites to clerk management — is creating internal chaos. Prospects and references describe the platform as clunky, support as terrible, and a company that "isn't listening."

iCompass

Legacy Meeting Management · Owned by Diligent · Being sunset in favor of Diligent Community

Win Theme: Modern vs. Legacy
Snapshot

iCompass is a legacy agenda and meeting management platform owned by Diligent. It was a strong product when implemented years ago, but development essentially stopped — the interface is largely unchanged from 2008. Diligent is now moving customers toward their "Diligent Community" platform, meaning iCompass is in de facto end-of-life status. Reps report they "don't believe iCompass is being actively sold anymore." The biggest wins against iCompass come from support quality, ease of use, and the platform's uncertain future.

Diligent-owned Strong in Ontario + some US markets Field-based reports (not Word)
Where They Win vs. Where They Lose
Where iCompass Wins Long-tenured customers with significant historical content archives and familiarity bias. Some organizations have been on the platform since 2008 and face inertia. Backed by Diligent's brand.
Where iCompass Loses Outdated interface that hasn't kept pace with modern expectations. "Not super user friendly" and "difficult to train people on." Field-based reports instead of Word-based. Product uncertainty — what is the future of this platform? Pricing increases at renewal pushing customers to shop.
Why eScribe Wins vs. iCompass
  • Modern, intuitive interface vs. a 2008-era UI that stopped evolving
  • Support quality is the #1 cited reason customers switch — "number one reason people move over to us"
  • Word-based reports and O365 integration vs. proprietary field-based forms
  • Better template customization and workflow flexibility
  • Public access embedded directly — no separate portal
  • Clear product roadmap vs. sunsetting platform with uncertain future
  • Three recent iCompass switches in a single month for one rep alone
Key Vulnerabilities to Probe
  • Have they heard that iCompass is being sunset by Diligent?
  • What happens to their data and workflows if the product is discontinued?
  • Has their renewal pricing increased significantly?
  • Are staff actually using the platform, or has adoption stalled?
  • Do their reports come out in Word format or proprietary fields?
  • Can the public access agendas directly from their website?
  • Are committees and boards fully on the system, or working around it?

CivicPlus

Municipal Website & Civic Engagement Platform · Meeting Management as an add-on · Owns Municode

Win Theme: Built for Meetings, Not Websites
Snapshot

CivicPlus is a broad municipal software vendor best known for websites, CMS, and code publishing (they own Municode). Agenda and meeting management is one of many products in their portfolio — and it shows. Their approach: "If we power your website, we'll throw in a meeting management module." The result is a product where meeting workflows feel secondary. Clerks describe agenda day as a full-calendar blocker. The product creates beautiful final output, but the process of getting there is painful. eScribe wins when the conversation shifts from "what's bundled" to "what actually works for clerks every day."

Website-first vendor Owns Municode (code publishing) Lower price point than eScribe CivicClerk product line
Where They Win vs. Where They Lose
Where CivicPlus Wins Price (often ~50% less than eScribe). Existing website relationship creates easy bundled conversations. Municode integration for automatic codification updates is a genuine differentiator for some prospects. Beautiful final agenda output.
Where CivicPlus Loses Cumbersome agenda creation — "every Thursday I clear my calendar because it's just agenda day." Making a document change marks the whole agenda as amended. Items edited like puzzle pieces — pulled in and out separately. Staff don't like it, known issues go unfixed, and meeting management remains clearly secondary to their website business.
Why eScribe Wins vs. CivicPlus
  • Purpose-built meeting workflows vs. a website module bolted on
  • Editing a document doesn't mark the whole agenda as amended
  • Last-minute document updates handled without chaos
  • Word and O365 integration that clerks actually enjoy using
  • Missing features in CivicPlus: surveys, term tracking — eScribe has them
  • Support that responds vs. known issues left unfixed
  • Demos convert easily — one demo reversed a CivicPlus decision for Vallejo Flood and Wastewater District
Key Vulnerabilities to Probe
  • How long does agenda prep take every week? Is a specific day blocked for it?
  • What happens when a document changes after the agenda is published?
  • Do they use the CivicPlus meeting module, or just their website tools?
  • Are staff actually using the system, or still going back to email and Word?
  • Do they use surveys, term tracking, or committee management? (CivicPlus lacks these)
  • Have they tried to get issues fixed — and how long did it take?

Govinity

Early-Stage Startup · Founded 2025 · "Operating System for Open Governance" · First users: Dec 2025

Win Theme: Proven Today vs. Wait and See
Snapshot

Govinity is a 2025 startup positioning itself as an "Operating System for Open Governance." The founding team has strong govtech and clerk-domain expertise, and their modern UI and branding are notable. But as of early 2026, the product is very early-stage — first real users were provisioned in December 2025. No third-party reviews exist on G2 or Capterra, and core features are still in development. Govinity exhibited at ICMA 2025 and is generating interest among innovation-forward agencies. The primary risk for any prospective customer: limited financial runway as a self-funded startup, unproven long-term operating model, and serious questions about data continuity if funding changes.

Paper / Email / PDF

Status Quo · Manual Processes · "We've always done it this way"

Win Theme: Control Without Complexity
Snapshot

The most common "competitor" in the market — especially for smaller municipalities and first-time buyers. Many organizations still run meetings through a combination of paper packets, email chains, Word documents, and PDF publishing. This works until it doesn't: version confusion during last-minute edits, no electronic voting, council members struggling to find the right document, and staff spending disproportionate time on manual assembly. The shift from paper/email/PDF to eScribe is typically prompted by council pressure to modernize, a new clerk looking to streamline, or a meeting that went badly due to document chaos.

Importantly, first-time buyers who move off manual processes often become eScribe's most enthusiastic references — because the productivity gain is immediate and dramatic.

Why Organizations Stay Manual
  • Fear of change — "we've always done it this way"
  • Concern about technology adoption among council members
  • Budget constraints or procurement complexity
  • Not sure what the software actually does vs. what they already have
  • Previous bad experience with overly complex tools
  • Underestimating the hidden cost of manual processes
Pain Points to Surface
  • "Agenda prep as a jumbled nightmare" — version confusion, email chains, last-minute changes
  • Everything lives in email and Word — nothing stays organized or findable
  • No electronic voting — tallies done manually, recorded by hand
  • Council members can't access agenda packets from their phones
  • Public transparency requires extra staff effort to publish and maintain
  • Council pressure to modernize coming from board members used to better tools
  • Audit and records compliance risk with no centralized system
Why eScribe Wins vs. Manual
  • Easy to adopt — designed to feel familiar from day one, minimal change management
  • Centralized agendas, minutes, and voting in one place
  • Electronic voting with instant, accurate results
  • Council members access packets from any device — no more printed stacks
  • Automatic public publishing — transparency without extra staff work
  • Immediate productivity gains for clerks on the very first meeting
  • First-time buyers become eScribe's most enthusiastic references

Talk Track
"Bring structure to meeting prep without overwhelming staff." Most first-time buyers are just looking for control — not a massive system. eScribe is designed to feel familiar from day one. Agenda prep replaces email chaos, not their entire workflow.
"Council pressure to modernize is real — but they don't want complicated." Surface the pressure first. Then position eScribe as the answer that's easy enough for the least tech-comfortable council member to adopt without training.
"What does your current agenda day actually look like?" Let them describe it. When they explain the email chains, the last-minute Word edits, the version confusion, the hand-tallied votes — that's when the value proposition answers itself.
